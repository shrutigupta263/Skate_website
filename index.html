The word skate has multiple definitions, including a sport, a type of shoe, and a verb: Skateboarding: A sport that involves riding a skateboard and performing tricks while standing on a flat board with four rollers attached. Skateboarding originated in California in the 1940s as an alternative to surfing when the waves were flat. It's considered an extreme sport and has professional competitions that include street-style and vertical events. Street-style events feature tricks performed in an urban environment with obstacles like stairs, rails, and ledges, while vertical skating, also known as "vert", involves aerial acrobatics in half-pipes. Skateboarding can be beneficial for balance, concentration, fitness, and creativity, and it's also a fun and environmentally friendly way to travel short distances.

Since the 1970s, skateparks have been constructed specifically for use by skateboarders, freestyle BMXers, aggressive skaters, and more recently, scooters.[5] However, skateboarding has become controversial in areas in which the activity, although legal, has damaged curbs, stoneworks, steps, benches, plazas, and parks.
